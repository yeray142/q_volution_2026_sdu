# Track D: Social Impact - Preparation Guide

**Sponsor:** Infleqtion UK

Welcome to the Quantum for Good track! Because this is a "No-Code / Design" track, there are no mandatory coding SDKs to install. 

However, **30% of your score is Conceptual Accuracy.** If you propose a quantum computer to do something a classical Macbook can already do (like running a database or a mobile app), the judges will deduct points. Quantum is not magic; it is highly specific math and physics. 

To help you build a *realistic* pitch, study these resources before you brainstorm:

**1. WHAT CAN QUANTUM COMPUTERS ACTUALLY DO?**
Quantum computers excel at two main things relevant to social impact:
*   **Combinatorial Optimization:** Solving complex logistics. Think disaster response routing, allocating medical resources during a pandemic, or optimizing traffic evacuation routes. 
    *   *Read this:* [McKinsey Report: Quantum Computing for the Planet](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/quantum-computing-just-might-save-the-planet)
*   **Molecular Simulation:** Simulating nature to invent new things. Think carbon capture materials, better battery chemistry, or eco-friendly agriculture.
    *   *Read this:* [Quantum Computing for Climate Change](https://www.weforum.org/publications/quantum-computing-for-climate-change)

**4. HOW TO STRUCTURE YOUR 5-SLIDE DECK**
Do not waste slides. Structure your PDF exactly like this:
*   **Slide 1: Crisis.** What is the specific real-world problem? (Use data/UN SDGs).
*   **Slide 2: Classical Bottleneck.** Why can't current classical supercomputers or sensors solve this today? (Explain the exact limitation).
*   **Slide 3: Quantum Solution.** Are you using Optimization, Simulation, or Sensing? How does it bypass the classical bottleneck?
*   **Slide 4: Social Impact.** If this works, how many lives are saved? What is the environmental metric? 
*   **Slide 5: The Roadmap.** What is required to make this real in the next 5-10 years?
